## Key Themes from Outline

### Process Today
- Word document RFP → hotels fill it in → manual transfer to Excel → errors occur
- Hotels give lengthy comments instead of simple yes/no → hard to parse
- Multiple contracts arrive simultaneously → hard to catch errors
- Rate discrepancies and contract errors are a real problem

### Top Priorities in RFPs (Anabel's ranking)
1. **Flexible cancellation policy** — #1 priority, learned from COVID. Hotels try to charge despite force majeure. Non-negotiable.
2. **Commission rates** — key financial item
3. **Meeting space** — crucial for operations
4. **Suite upgrades** — complex chain of command; multiple suites needed for different team members; errors impact budget
5. **Playoff clauses** — teams need clauses in different cities; some teams prioritize this, others don't

### Team Decision-Making / Hotel Selection
- Criteria vary by team (e.g. Cavaliers have specific preferences)
- Some teams prioritize budget; others focus on amenities or postseason
- Playoff clauses affect hotel choices city by city

### Scoring / Prioritization
- Team discussed creating an internal hotel score based on: commission, meeting space, suite upgrades, flexible cancellation
- Anabel confirmed those are the right criteria

### Competitor Intel
- **Pivot** is a competitor that offered significant marketing funds → led to teams switching and increased travel expenses for KJST clients
- Prior-year spend data from teams that left Pivot could be valuable for marketing comparisons

### Baseball Expansion
- Planning to use the new tool for baseball RFPs in the coming months (2–3 teams)
- Baseball has high RFP volume — system needs to be flexible and efficient

### Handling Emergencies
- Last-minute weather delays, unexpected room needs, meal coordination
- Team works directly with hotels for emergency solutions
- Coordination involves hotels, teams, and league officials
